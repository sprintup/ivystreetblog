# Booklists User Guide

## Overview

The bookshelf side of Ivy Street is for readers and curators.

It helps you:

- save books into your personal collection
- organize those books into booklists
- browse public booklists made by other users
- keep a private reading list of books you want to read later

This side of the app is mobile friendly, but parental supervision is still advised when choosing books for children.

## Main Areas

### My Collection

`My Collection` is your personal pool of books.

- A book can live in your collection without being in any booklist.
- Once a book is in your collection, you can place it into one or more booklists.
- You can also add books from public booklists into your own collection and then organize them further.

Use `My Collection` when you want to keep your own copy of a book record before deciding where it belongs.

### My Bookshelf

`My Bookshelf` is where you manage your booklists.

- Create a new booklist with `Add Booklist`.
- Edit a booklist to change its title, description, visibility, and included books.
- Keep a booklist private while you are still building it.
- Make a booklist public when you want it to appear on the public bookshelf.

Each booklist can act like a themed recommendation shelf, such as:

- bedtime stories
- books about weather
- favorite read-alouds
- early nonfiction picks

### Public Bookshelf

`Public Bookshelf` is the discovery area.

- Browse public booklists created by other users.
- Open a public booklist to see all the books in it.
- If you are logged in, add interesting books to your reading list or one of your own booklists.
- Visit the curator's public profile from a public booklist to see their other public shelves.

Use the public bookshelf when you want inspiration without having to build a list from scratch.

### Reading List

`My Reading List` is your private save-for-later tool.

- Add a book when you want to remember it quickly.
- Use it as a short-term holding place before deciding whether the book belongs in a permanent booklist.
- Remove a book from the reading list when you are no longer interested in it.

This is especially useful when you are browsing public shelves on your phone and want to save books quickly.

## Typical Workflow

One simple pattern is:

1. Browse the `Public Bookshelf`.
2. Add interesting books to your `Reading List`.
3. Move your best finds into `My Collection`.
4. Build a themed booklist in `My Bookshelf`.
5. Make that booklist public when you are ready to share it.

## Recommendations

Some public booklists are open to recommendations.

- When a public booklist accepts recommendations, logged-in users can suggest books from their own collection.
- The booklist owner reviews those recommendations and decides whether to accept them.

This lets public lists improve over time without giving other users direct edit access.

## Public Profile

Your public profile name is what other people use to find your public bookshelf.

- Keep it non-identifying.
- Use something neutral, memorable, and safe to share.
- When people click your public profile name on a public booklist, they can browse your other public booklists.

## Mobile Tips

- The app works well for quick browsing on a phone.
- The reading list is often the easiest place to save books when you are away from your desk.
- If a page has a lot of books, use the pagination controls to move through the collection without overwhelming the layout.

## Good First Steps

If you are new to the bookshelf features:

1. Add a few books to `My Collection`.
2. Create one small booklist on `My Bookshelf`.
3. Browse the `Public Bookshelf`.
4. Save a few books to `My Reading List`.
5. Decide which books are worth keeping in a long-term booklist.
